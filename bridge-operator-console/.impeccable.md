## Design Context

### Users

Bridge operators and integrators running the ZK-Stables relayer stack locally or in staging: they monitor chain health, confirm payout identities, and either drive `bridge-cli` or post HTTP intents after an EVM lock or burn is on-chain.

### Brand Personality

**Precise, archival, kinetic** — reads like a well-made instrument panel: calm paper field, sharp typographic hierarchy, color used sparingly to signal direction of value (mint vs redeem), never decorative noise.

### Aesthetic Direction

Light, editorial-meets-control-room: warm paper neutrals tinted toward amber, deep blue-ink typography, ember accent for “value into zk”, steel blue for “value back to EVM”. No dark-mode-with-glow default, no cyan–purple gradients, no gradient text. Pair **Bricolage Grotesque** (headings) with **Literata** (body) for contrast without cliché “tech” monospace.

### Design Principles

1. **One job per surface** — Accounts = read-only truth from the relayer; Bridge = intent construction and submit, not mixed with health chrome.
2. **Honest affordances** — If the browser cannot sign the pool lock, the UI says so and gives an exact `zk-bridge` command plus optional POST when anchors exist.
3. **Rhythm over boxes** — Group with spacing and typographic weight; borders are hairlines, not card stacks.
4. **Accessible motion** — Prefer opacity and subtle translate; respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
